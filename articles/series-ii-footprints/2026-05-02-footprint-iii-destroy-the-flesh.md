# Footprint III: Destroy the Flesh

## Opening Scripture

“Remember Lot’s wife.” — Luke 17:32

---

## Body

Footprint III is where the believer stops negotiating with the old self.

After abiding in Christ and obeying the Word, the next battle becomes unavoidable:

Destroy the flesh.

Jesus said, “Remember Lot’s wife.”

That warning is not only about a woman who looked back. It is about a heart that escaped judgment physically while still desiring the place God delivered it from spiritually.

She left Sodom with her feet.

But her heart was still there.

That is the danger.

A person can leave the old life outwardly while still longing for it inwardly. A person can speak the language of deliverance while still protecting the appetite of bondage. A person can move forward in public while secretly looking backward in desire.

Footprint III says no.

Do not look back.

Do not romanticize the sinful past.

Do not turn the old life into nostalgia.

Do not keep fellowship with what Christ died to free you from.

The flesh does not want to die. The flesh wants a compromise. It wants a secret room. It wants a backup plan. It wants to obey the Word only where obedience is convenient. It wants to abide in Christ publicly while feeding the old appetite privately.

But Jesus did not say manage the flesh.

He said deny yourself.

Take up your cross.

Follow Him.

That means something must die.

Not your calling.

Not your purpose.

Not your future.

But the old self.

The pride.

The lust.

The bitterness.

The rebellion.

The appetite for sin.

The need for applause.

The love of the old crowd.

The desire to be seen.

The version of you that wanted God’s blessing without God’s authority.

That one cannot walk the Footprints of the Messiah.

That one must be crucified.

Destroy the flesh does not mean hatred of the body. The body belongs to God. It means refusing the sinful nature that wars against the Spirit. It means refusing to let the old self rule the new life.

Paul teaches that the flesh and the Spirit are opposed to one another. The believer cannot feed both and expect peace. One will lead. One will rule. One will shape the life.

So Footprint III asks:

What are you still feeding?

What are you still protecting?

What old appetite have you renamed instead of crucified?

What past relationship, habit, identity, or desire keeps calling you back?

What part of Sodom still lives in the heart?

This is why Luke 17:32 leads this sermon.

Remember Lot’s wife.

Remember what happens when deliverance begins but desire looks backward.

Remember what happens when the body exits but the heart remains attached.

Remember what happens when God opens a way forward and the soul grieves the city of sin.

Some people cannot come with you into obedience.

That does not mean you hate them.

It means you cannot let their sin become your chain.

You can love people.

You can pray for people.

You can witness to people.

But you cannot let the crowd God delivered you from disciple you again.

There comes a point where the believer must say:

I cannot go back there.

I cannot live like that anymore.

I cannot laugh at what crucified my Savior.

I cannot entertain what poisoned my soul.

I cannot walk with Jesus while keeping agreement with darkness.

And when evil tries to pull you backward, the believer must answer with holy seriousness:

Away from me.

Not because you are better.

Because you have been bought.

Not because you are perfect.

Because you have surrendered.

Not because you never sinned.

Because you refuse to make peace with sin anymore.

Jesus warned that not everyone who says “Lord, Lord” truly knows Him. Some will point to religious activity, public works, and spiritual language. But Christ can still say, “I never knew you; depart from Me, you workers of lawlessness.”

That means religious activity cannot cover an unsurrendered life.

The issue is not noise.

The issue is Lordship.

Did you surrender?

Did you obey?

Did you let Christ kill the old man?

Did you let the Spirit reform the appetite?

Did you let the Word rule the private places?

Footprint III is not about pretending there is no past.

It is about refusing to let the past become master.

The past can become testimony.

But it cannot become throne.

The old self can be buried.

But it cannot be consulted.

The sinful past can be remembered as evidence of mercy.

But it cannot be revisited as home.

This is the first severe Footprint.

Abide connected the believer to Christ.

Obey submitted the believer to the Word.

Destroy the Flesh now removes the internal opposition to both.

Because the flesh will resist abiding.

The flesh will resist obedience.

The flesh will resist prayer.

The flesh will resist holiness.

The flesh will resist the Fifth Gospel because the Fifth Gospel requires the life to become readable.

A flesh-led life reveals the flesh.

A Spirit-led life reveals Christ.

So Footprint III declares:

I am not going back.

I am not looking back.

I am not feeding the old self.

I am not protecting the appetite God exposed.

I am not returning to what Christ delivered me from.

I am walking forward.

Series I brought the believer through Surrender → Mirror → Water → Fire → Abide in Christ.

Series II now requires the believer to walk what was formed.

The next step after destroying the flesh is not emptiness.

It is replacement.

Walk in the Spirit.

Because whatever is destroyed must be replaced by life in God.
