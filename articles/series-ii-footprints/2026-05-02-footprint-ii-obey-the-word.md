# Footprint II: Obey the Word

## Opening Scripture

“But be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves.” — James 1:22

---

## Body

Footprint II is Obey the Word.

After the believer abides in Christ, the next step is not inspiration.

It is obedience.

The Word of God was never given to be admired only. It was given to be lived. A person can quote Scripture, study Scripture, record Scripture, post Scripture, and still resist Scripture if the life refuses to obey.

That is why James warns the church:

Be doers of the Word, and not hearers only.

Hearing without obedience creates deception. It allows a person to feel spiritual because truth has been heard, while the life remains unchanged. But the Fifth Gospel is not written by hearing alone. The Fifth Gospel is written when the life begins to obey what the mouth confesses.

Jesus said, “If you love Me, keep My commandments.”

Love is not proven only by emotion.

Love is proven by obedience.

That does not mean the believer is saved by works. It means the saved life becomes obedient because Christ is Lord. Obedience is not the root of salvation. It is the fruit of surrender.

This is why Footprint II comes after Abide in Christ.

Obedience without abiding becomes legalism.

Abiding without obedience becomes sentiment.

But abiding that leads to obedience becomes discipleship.

The believer remains in Christ, listens to Christ, and then walks in the Word of Christ.

This is where faith becomes visible.

To obey the Word means the believer stops treating Scripture as a suggestion. It means the Word of God becomes the authority over thought, speech, action, family, ambition, work, platform, calling, and witness.

The Word corrects.

The Word commands.

The Word comforts.

The Word confronts.

The Word sends.

The Word restrains.

The Word forms.

The Word becomes the measure of the life.

That is the walk of the Messiah.

Jesus obeyed the Father perfectly. He did not come to perform His own will apart from the Father. He spoke what the Father gave Him. He did what the Father sent Him to do. His life was total obedience.

The disciple cannot claim to follow Christ while refusing the path of obedience.

This is where the sermon becomes personal.

If Christ spread the Word of God perfectly, then the disciple must spread the Word faithfully. Not perfectly like Christ, but faithfully through Christ. The believer may not have the same ability, reach, platform, strength, or clarity, but obedience is measured by surrender, not comparison.

If the Word says teach, teach.

If the Word says pray, pray.

If the Word says forgive, forgive.

If the Word says repent, repent.

If the Word says go, go.

If the Word says speak, speak.

If the Word says love, love.

If the Word says deny yourself, deny yourself.

If the Word says make disciples, make disciples.

Obey the Word.

This is not performance for applause.

This is submission to Christ.

The believer who obeys the Word becomes readable. The world may not understand every doctrine, but it can read obedience. It can read consistency. It can read humility. It can read repentance. It can read whether the life is being shaped by Scripture or by appetite.

Footprint II also protects the archive.

The purpose of these articles is not merely to store religious thoughts. The purpose is to preserve a witness that points back to the Word. If the archive grows but obedience shrinks, the system becomes hollow.

The Word must remain above the work.

The Word must remain above the platform.

The Word must remain above the brand.

The Word must remain above the archive.

The Word must remain above the teacher.

This is why obedience is holy.

It breaks self-rule.

It defeats selective Christianity.

It refuses the lie that a person can follow Christ while ignoring His commands.

Obedience is where faith gains weight.

The believer says:

I will not only hear.

I will do.

I will not only agree.

I will obey.

I will not only speak.

I will live.

I will not only study.

I will walk.

This is the second Footprint because abiding gives the believer connection, but obedience gives the walk direction.

Without obedience, the believer remains inspired but unmoved.

With obedience, the believer becomes the Fifth Gospel in motion.

A life obeying the Word becomes a living sermon.

A family obeying the Word becomes a witness.

A platform obeying the Word becomes ministry.

A doctrine obeying the Word becomes formation.

A public life obeying the Word becomes readable to those who may never open Scripture first.

That is the burden and the blessing.

People will read your life.

So obey the Word.

Let the Word shape your mouth.

Let the Word shape your schedule.

Let the Word shape your repentance.

Let the Word shape your teaching.

Let the Word shape your witness.

Let the Word shape your next step.

Footprint I said remain in Christ.

Footprint II says obey His Word.

And obedience will soon require a deeper battle:

the flesh must be destroyed.

Because the old self will resist the Word.

The old appetite will argue with the Word.

The old life will try to reinterpret the Word to protect itself.

So after obedience is established, the next Footprint becomes necessary.

Destroy the Flesh.

But first, the believer must settle this:

The Word is not optional.

Christ is Lord.

And the disciple must obey.
