# Footprint I: Abide in Christ

## Opening Scripture

“Abide in Me, and I in you.” — John 15:4

---

## Body

The first Footprint of the Messiah is not action.

It is connection.

Before the believer obeys publicly, teaches boldly, destroys the flesh, walks in the Spirit, endures the fire, reflects Christ, or multiplies the witness, the believer must first abide in Christ.

This is where Series II begins.

Series I formed the soul through Surrender → Mirror → Water → Fire → Abide in Christ. Now Series II asks what the formed believer does next.

The answer is not to run.

The answer is to remain.

Jesus said, “Abide in Me, and I in you.”

That means the Christian life cannot be lived by distance. The disciple does not borrow Christ’s name and then operate in flesh-power. The disciple remains attached to Christ the way a branch remains attached to the vine.

Without the vine, the branch produces nothing.

Not less fruit.

No fruit.

That is why abiding is Footprint I.

It is the root beneath every other step.

Abiding means prayer is not optional.

Abiding means Scripture is not decoration.

Abiding means obedience flows from relationship.

Abiding means the believer is not performing Christianity for the crowd but remaining connected to Christ in private.

Jesus Himself withdrew to pray. With healing to do, teaching to give, people to reach, opposition to answer, and miracles surrounding Him, He still went away to be with the Father.

That is the pattern.

If Jesus made time for prayer, the disciple has no excuse for prayerless movement.

If Jesus withdrew from the crowd, the disciple cannot be ruled by visibility.

If Jesus remained aligned with the Father, the believer cannot walk the Footprints of the Messiah apart from intimacy with Christ.

Abiding protects the witness.

Without abiding, obedience becomes legalism.

Without abiding, destroying the flesh becomes self-discipline without grace.

Without abiding, walking in the Spirit becomes religious language.

Without abiding, enduring the fire becomes survival instead of refinement.

Without abiding, reflecting Christ publicly becomes performance.

Without abiding, multiplying the witness becomes expansion without life.

Everything depends on the vine.

This is also why abiding follows Series I and begins Series II. It is both the end of formation and the beginning of movement. The believer is formed by surrender, examined by the Mirror, cleansed by Water, refined by Fire, and then rooted in Christ.

Only then can the walk begin.

To abide is to stay.

Stay when the emotions fade.

Stay when the numbers are low.

Stay when the crowd does not understand.

Stay when the old life calls.

Stay when fire tests the motive.

Stay when obedience costs something.

Stay when God is forming what no one else can see.

The Fifth Gospel is not written by occasional religious excitement. It is written by daily remaining. People do not only read what a believer says. They read what a believer stays connected to.

If the life is connected to pride, people read pride.

If the life is connected to fear, people read fear.

If the life is connected to performance, people read performance.

But if the life is connected to Christ, people begin to read Christ.

That is the purpose of the Fifth Gospel.

Not to replace Matthew, Mark, Luke, or John.

But to let the life become a living witness to the Christ those Gospels reveal.

Footprint I says:

Do not move before you abide.

Do not teach before you pray.

Do not preach before you listen.

Do not build before you remain.

Do not let the platform outrun the altar.

Do not let visibility replace intimacy.

The old self wants to be seen.

The abiding self wants to be rooted.

The old self wants applause.

The abiding self wants Christ.

The old self wants output.

The abiding self wants fruit.

Fruit is different from output. Output can be produced by effort. Fruit comes from life. The branch does not force grapes into existence. The branch remains connected, and life flows.

That is the mystery of abiding.

The believer becomes fruitful by remaining faithful.

This is the first step because every other step will test this one. Obeying the Word will test whether the believer abides. Destroying the flesh will test whether the believer abides. Walking in the Spirit will test whether the believer abides. Enduring the fire will test whether the believer abides.

So the first command is simple:

Remain.

Stay with Christ.

Return to Christ.

Rest in Christ.

Pray with Christ.

Listen to Christ.

Obey from Christ.

The Footprints of the Messiah begin here because the Messiah Himself is not merely the example.

He is the source.

To follow His footprints, the believer must remain with Him.

This is Footprint I.

Abide in Christ.

Everything else flows from here.
