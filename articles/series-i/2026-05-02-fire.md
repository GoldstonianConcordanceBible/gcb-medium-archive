# Fire

## Opening Scripture

“That the genuineness of your faith… may be found to praise, honor, and glory at the revelation of Jesus Christ.” — 1 Peter 1:7

---

## Body

After Water comes Fire.

Water cleanses.

Fire refines.

Water washes what was exposed.

Fire tests what remains.

This is where many believers misunderstand God. They think cleansing means the process is over. They think forgiveness means formation is finished. They think because God washed them, nothing difficult should follow.

But Scripture teaches otherwise.

After surrender, after the Mirror, after Water, there is Fire.

Not the fire of condemnation.

The fire of refinement.

Fire is where faith becomes proven. It is where the believer discovers whether obedience was only emotional or truly rooted. It is where the old self, already exposed and washed, is tested again under pressure.

Because anything untested can still be theoretical.

Fire reveals what remains when comfort is removed.

Fire reveals whether surrender was real.

Fire reveals whether the soul obeys God only when obedience feels easy.

Fire reveals whether the believer wants Christ or only His benefits.

This is why Fire belongs before Abide in Christ.

A person cannot truly abide if every trial disconnects them. A person cannot remain in Christ if every heat wave sends them back to the old life. Fire teaches endurance. Fire teaches dependence. Fire teaches the believer to stay attached to Christ when the flesh screams for escape.

The refining fire does not come to destroy what belongs to God.

It comes to remove what does not.

Gold is not afraid of fire because fire reveals its purity. But mixture fears fire because fire separates.

That is the work of God.

The fire separates faith from performance.

Obedience from emotion.

Calling from ego.

Witness from attention-seeking.

Holiness from image.

The Fifth Gospel must pass through Fire because public witness without refinement becomes dangerous. A person can preach truth while still being ruled by pride. A person can speak Scripture while still craving applause. A person can build a platform while still avoiding crucifixion of the old self.

Fire burns the mixture.

Fire asks:

Will you still obey when nobody claps?

Will you still pray when nobody sees?

Will you still forgive when pride wants revenge?

Will you still teach when the numbers are low?

Will you still follow when the path costs you?

Will you still trust when God does not explain everything?

That is Fire.

Fire is not just suffering.

Fire is purposeful testing under the hand of God.

The believer who has passed through Mirror and Water must now be strengthened. Cleansing removes defilement. Refinement produces durability. God does not merely want a forgiven life. He wants a faithful life.

A life that can stand.

A life that can endure.

A life that can witness under pressure.

A life that can become readable even in trial.

This is where the old self tries one more time to return. The flesh says, “This is too hard.” The past says, “Come back.” The enemy says, “God has abandoned you.” But Fire teaches the believer to distinguish pain from punishment.

Not every painful process is rejection.

Sometimes pain is refinement.

Sometimes the heat means God is preparing the life to carry more weight.

Sometimes the pressure means the testimony is becoming stronger.

Sometimes the fire means what was cleansed is now being purified for use.

This is why the believer must not run from Fire.

Fire is where the sermon becomes real.

Fire is where doctrine becomes life.

Fire is where the archive becomes witness.

Fire is where the Fifth Gospel becomes legible under pressure.

The world does not only read your life when you are blessed. It reads your life when you are tested. It watches how you respond when things are hard. It watches whether you still obey. It watches whether Christ remains Lord when comfort is gone.

That is why Fire is sacred.

Not easy.

Sacred.

The surrendered believer can now say:

Lord, refine what remains.

Burn away what cannot follow.

Remove the mixture.

Purify the motive.

Strengthen the witness.

Make my life true.

This is not self-destruction.

This is consecration.

God is a consuming fire, but His fire is holy. It does not randomly burn. It reveals, separates, purifies, and prepares.

Series I reaches its intensity here.

Salvation was questioned.

The soul wrestled.

God’s goodness was confessed.

Surrender opened the doorway.

Mirror revealed the truth.

Water cleansed what was exposed.

Now Fire refines what remains.

And after Fire comes the only way to continue:

Abide in Christ.

Because no one survives holy formation by willpower alone.

The believer must remain connected to the Vine.

Fire prepares the soul to understand this.

You cannot live the Christian life detached from Christ.

You cannot walk the Footprints through flesh-power.

You cannot become the Fifth Gospel through discipline alone.

You must abide.

Fire burns away the illusion that you can do this without Him.

That is mercy.

So let the fire do its work.

Let it purify the witness.

Let it test the faith.

Let it burn the mixture.

Let it prepare the life.

Because after the Fire, the believer is not called to run ahead.

The believer is called to remain.
