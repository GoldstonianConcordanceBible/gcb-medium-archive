# Surrender

## Opening Scripture

“If anyone desires to come after Me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross daily, and follow Me.” — Luke 9:23

---

## Body

Surrender is the doorway.

Before Mirror.

Before Water.

Before Fire.

Before Abide in Christ.

Before the Footprints of the Messiah.

There must be surrender.

Surrender is where the old throne is abandoned. It is where the soul stops asking God to bless self-rule and begins to bow before Christ as Lord. It is not partial agreement. It is not religious inspiration. It is not emotional excitement after a sermon.

Surrender is the death of ownership.

It says:

My life is not mine.

My future is not mine.

My ambition is not mine.

My platform is not mine.

My family is not mine.

My gifts are not mine.

My pain is not mine.

My testimony is not mine.

All of it belongs to Christ.

This is why surrender is difficult. The flesh does not mind religion as long as it remains in control. The old self can attend church, quote Scripture, post faith content, and still refuse the Lordship of Jesus Christ. But surrender removes the disguise. It asks the question beneath every question:

Who rules you?

Not who inspires you.

Not who comforts you.

Not who you mention publicly.

Who rules you?

Jesus did not call people to admire Him from a distance. He called them to follow Him. And following Him means denying the self that wants to remain king. It means taking up the cross daily. It means walking behind Christ, not asking Christ to walk behind your plan.

That is the break.

Surrender is not saying, “God, help me accomplish my will.”

Surrender is saying, “Father, Your will be done.”

This is the turning point of Series I.

The first article warned against false assurance.

The second article brought the soul into wrestling.

The third article declared the goodness of God.

Now the soul must respond.

If God is good, surrender is safe.

If Christ is Lord, surrender is required.

If the Spirit is holy, surrender is the beginning of formation.

Without surrender, the Mirror will be avoided. Without surrender, the Water will be resisted. Without surrender, the Fire will be misunderstood. Without surrender, abiding becomes language instead of life.

But with surrender, everything changes.

The Mirror becomes mercy because it shows what must be healed.

The Water becomes grace because it cleanses what must be removed.

The Fire becomes refinement because it burns what cannot remain.

Abiding becomes life because the believer finally stops trying to live apart from Christ.

Surrender is not weakness.

It is alignment.

It is the soul returning to the authority of its Creator.

It is the believer saying, “I no longer belong to the old life.”

Paul said, “I have been crucified with Christ; it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me.”

That is surrender.

Not improvement.

Crucifixion.

Not decoration.

Death and resurrection.

The old self cannot be coached into holiness. It must be crucified with Christ. The old appetite cannot be negotiated into obedience. It must be brought under the Lordship of Christ. The old life cannot become the Fifth Gospel. It must die so Christ can be seen.

This is where many turn away.

They want Jesus as Savior but not King. They want forgiveness but not obedience. They want blessing but not surrender. They want heaven but not the cross.

But the way of Christ has always been clear:

Deny yourself.

Take up your cross.

Follow Me.

Surrender is the moment when the believer stops editing the command.

It is where faith becomes total.

Not the public part only.

Not the religious part only.

Not the comfortable part only.

All of it.

This is the foundation of the GCB Medium Archive and the Professor Enoch layer. The purpose is not to build a platform of words while the heart remains unsurrendered. The purpose is to preserve a witness that points back to Christ.

The archive must begin where discipleship begins:

at the feet of Jesus.

Surrender means Christ has authority over the content, the calling, the correction, the timing, the ambition, and the outcome.

Surrender means the believer does not use God to build self.

Surrender means God uses the life to reveal Christ.

And once surrender happens, the real formation can begin.

Now the soul is ready for the Mirror.

Not because it is strong.

Because it has bowed.

Not because it is perfect.

Because it is submitted.

Not because it understands everything.

Because it trusts the One who is good.

Series I turns here.

The doorway has opened.

The old throne has been abandoned.

The believer is no longer asking Christ to assist the old life.

The believer is ready to lose that life and find the true one in Him.

This is surrender.

This is the doorway.

This is where the formation before the Footprints truly begins.
