# God Is Good / Dios Es Bueno / Elohim Tov

## Opening Scripture

“Oh, give thanks to the LORD, for He is good! For His mercy endures forever.” — Psalm 107:1

---

## Body

Before surrender becomes complete, the heart must recognize the goodness of God.

This article stands in Series I because many people resist surrender not because they hate God, but because they do not fully trust His goodness. They fear what He may take. They fear what He may change. They fear that surrender means loss instead of life.

But the Word declares:

God is good.

Dios es bueno.

Elohim tov.

This is not just language. This is recognition.

To say God is good in more than one tongue is to confess that His goodness is not trapped inside one culture, one nation, one church tradition, or one personal experience. His goodness stands above language while still entering language. His mercy can be spoken in English, Spanish, Hebrew, and every tongue that will one day confess that Jesus Christ is Lord.

But this phrase must become more than sound.

It must become surrender.

Because if God is truly good, then His will is not a threat. His correction is not cruelty. His commandments are not chains. His fire is not destruction. His water is not humiliation. His mirror is not condemnation.

They are mercy.

The goodness of God is what makes surrender possible.

A person cannot fully surrender to a God they secretly mistrust. They may obey outwardly. They may perform religiously. They may speak correctly. But deep inside, the old self will keep one hand on control.

That is why this sermon comes before Surrender in the deeper formation logic.

The heart must learn that God is not trying to rob it.

God is trying to redeem it.

God is not trying to erase your life.

God is trying to resurrect it.

God is not trying to silence your purpose.

God is trying to purify it.

God is not trying to destroy the true you.

God is trying to destroy the false self that sin built around you.

When we say “God is good,” we are not saying life is easy. We are not saying pain is imaginary. We are not saying suffering disappears. We are saying that beneath every trial, beyond every season, above every enemy, and through every refining fire, the character of God remains good.

That is why this confession matters.

The old self says, “Protect yourself.”

Faith says, “God is good.”

The flesh says, “Keep control.”

Faith says, “Dios es bueno.”

Fear says, “Do not let go.”

Faith says, “Elohim tov.”

This is the moment where language becomes worship.

And worship becomes formation.

And formation prepares the soul to surrender.

Series I is not rushing the reader into public witness. It is building the foundation beneath the witness. Before the believer can walk the Footprints of the Messiah, the believer must know the One whose footprints are being followed.

If God is good, then obedience is not oppression.

If God is good, then repentance is not shame.

If God is good, then cleansing is not punishment.

If God is good, then fire is not abandonment.

If God is good, then abiding is not limitation.

It is life.

This is why the Fifth Gospel cannot be built on fear. Your life cannot reveal Christ clearly if your heart believes God is unsafe. The readable life begins when the soul trusts the Author.

God is good.

That means His Word is good.

His timing is good.

His correction is good.

His mercy is good.

His holiness is good.

His call is good.

His plan is good.

Even when the process hurts, God is good.

Even when the mirror exposes, God is good.

Even when the water cleanses what you wanted to keep, God is good.

Even when the fire burns away what cannot remain, God is good.

Even when He tells you to leave the old life behind, God is good.

This article becomes the bridge between wrestling and surrender. The soul has been confronted. The night has revealed the struggle. Now the believer must decide whether the God who wrestles, wounds, renames, and blesses is worthy of trust.

The answer of Scripture is yes.

The answer of faith is yes.

The answer of the Fifth Gospel must become yes.

Because the world does not only need people who say God is good.

The world needs lives that prove they believe it.

A surrendered life is the evidence.

A cleansed life is the evidence.

A refined life is the evidence.

An abiding life is the evidence.

A public witness is the evidence.

So let this confession become more than a phrase.

Let it become the ground beneath your obedience.

God is good.

Dios es bueno.

Elohim tov.

And because He is good, the believer can surrender all.
