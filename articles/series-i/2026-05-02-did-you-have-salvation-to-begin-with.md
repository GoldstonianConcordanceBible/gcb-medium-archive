# Did You Have Salvation to Begin With?

## Opening Scripture

“Not everyone who says to Me, ‘Lord, Lord,’ shall enter the kingdom of heaven…” — Matthew 7:21

---

## Body

This is where Series I begins.

Not with confidence.

Not with performance.

Not with religious language.

But with the most dangerous question a believer can ask:

Did you have salvation to begin with?

This is not meant to condemn the sincere heart. This is meant to awaken the sleeping one. Because Jesus did not warn unbelievers only. He warned people who knew how to speak the language of faith. People who said “Lord, Lord.” People who could point to works, activity, and religious evidence.

Yet Christ still said, “I never knew you.”

That means the issue is not whether you can sound saved.

The issue is whether your life has surrendered.

Many people agree with Jesus but have not yielded to Jesus. They admire Him but do not obey Him. They call Him Savior but resist Him as Lord. They want forgiveness without transformation, blessing without surrender, and heaven without the death of the old self.

That is why this sermon belongs at the beginning.

Before Mirror.

Before Water.

Before Fire.

Before the Footprints of the Messiah.

There must be a holy confrontation.

Because the Fifth Gospel cannot be lived by a false convert. A person cannot reflect Christ publicly while refusing Christ privately. A person cannot obey the Word while using the Word as decoration. A person cannot walk in the Spirit while protecting the flesh.

Salvation is not a costume.

Salvation is not cultural membership.

Salvation is not family tradition.

Salvation is not saying the right words while keeping your own throne.

True salvation produces surrender. Not perfection, but surrender. Not instant maturity, but a new Lord. Not a sinless life, but a life that can no longer make peace with sin.

This is the difference between agreement and conversion.

Agreement says, “Jesus is true.”

Conversion says, “Jesus is Lord.”

Agreement adds Jesus to the old life.

Conversion buries the old life and rises in Christ.

Agreement wants Jesus nearby.

Conversion gives Jesus authority.

This is why Series I is called Surrender to Fire. The believer must be formed before the believer can be sent. The soul must be confronted before it can be cleansed. The old self must be exposed before it can be destroyed.

The first question is not, “How do I walk like Christ?”

The first question is, “Have I surrendered to Christ?”

Because without surrender, Mirror becomes self-help. Water becomes emotion. Fire becomes performance. Abiding becomes religious language. And the Footprints become a checklist instead of a life.

But when salvation is real, everything changes.

The Mirror no longer exists to shame you. It reveals what Christ is ready to heal.

The Water no longer exists as symbolism only. It becomes cleansing, repentance, and reformation.

The Fire no longer exists as punishment. It becomes refinement.

And Abide in Christ becomes the only way to remain alive in the new life God has given.

This sermon is the warning at the gate.

Do not build a public witness on a private lie.

Do not confuse content with conversion.

Do not confuse knowledge with surrender.

Do not confuse activity with intimacy.

Jesus is not impressed by religious noise when the heart remains lawless.

So the first act of the GCB Medium Archive is not to publish more words.

It is to ask the reader to stop.

Look inward.

Stand before Christ.

And ask:

Am I surrendered?

Do I know Him?

Does He know me?

Am I a hearer only, or a doer of the Word?

Because the Fifth Gospel begins when the life becomes readable. And the life becomes readable only when Christ becomes Lord over all of it.

This is Series I.

This is the beginning.

This is the warning before the walk.
