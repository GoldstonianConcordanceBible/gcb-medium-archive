# After Sermon 004: Water

## What God Showed Me After I Preached

Opening Scripture:

"Wash me, and I shall be whiter than snow." — Psalm 51:7

---

## What I Was Trying to Say

This sermon was about the water.

After the mirror shows me the truth, I cannot stay there staring at what is wrong.

I have to bring my whole self to God.

The water is cleansing.

The water is repentance.

The water is the place where God washes what the mirror exposed.

I was trying to say that God does not reveal corruption, pride, idolatry, fear, or disobedience just so I can feel bad.

He reveals it so I can be cleansed.

The mirror exposes.

The water washes.

---

## What I Actually Lived While Preaching It

While I was preaching, I felt the weight of how much God has been correcting in me.

Not just public things.

Private things.

Thoughts.

Motives.

Impatience.

Pride.

The desire to be understood.

The desire to be seen.

The water sermon reminded me that I cannot cleanse myself by effort alone.

I have to let Christ wash me.

I have to bring my whole entire self to God the Father, through the blood of Jesus Christ, in faith by the Holy Spirit.

The Triune God does the cleansing.

---

## What I Got Right

I believe I got the movement right.

Water comes after Mirror.

You cannot wash what you refuse to see.

I also believe I got the hope right.

Cleansing is not punishment.

Cleansing is mercy.

God does not expose me to leave me dirty.

He exposes me so He can wash me.

---

## What I Should Have Corrected

I should have made clearer that cleansing is not just emotional relief.

It is transformation.

The goal is not simply to feel better after conviction.

The goal is to become more obedient.

I also should have made clearer that cleansing requires surrender.

I cannot ask God to wash me while still clinging to the very thing He is trying to remove.

---

## What I Prayed About Afterward

Lord,

Wash what the mirror exposed.

Cleanse what I cannot cleanse by myself.

Remove pride.

Remove fear.

Remove hidden idolatry.

Remove anything in me that resists Your Word.

Do not let me confuse conviction with condemnation.

Let conviction lead me back to You.

Wash me in the blood of Jesus Christ.

Renew me by the Holy Spirit.

In Jesus’ name,

Amen.

---

## What God Is Teaching Me Now

God is teaching me that Water is mercy.

Surrender lets me come to God.

Abiding in Christ keeps me close to God.

Mirror shows me what needs correction.

Water teaches me that correction is not the end.

Cleansing is the next step.

Then comes Fire.

But I cannot endure the fire if I refuse the water.

The water prepares me to be refined.

---

## The Fifth Gospel Reflection

The Fifth Gospel cannot be lived through an unwashed witness.

If people are reading my life, then they need to see more than conviction.

They need to see cleansing.

They need to see that God can take a man, expose him, wash him, and keep forming him.

That is testimony.

That is the life after the sermon.

---

## Closing Witness

I preached the sermon once.

Now I have to live it daily.
