# After Sermon 001: Surrender

## What God Showed Me After I Preached

Opening Scripture:

"Not my will, but yours, be done." — Luke 22:42

---

## What I Was Trying to Say

This sermon was about surrender.

Not partial surrender.

Not religious surrender.

Not the kind of surrender where I give God the parts of my life I already planned to give Him.

Real surrender means Christ wants all of me.

My plans.

My pride.

My platform.

My calling.

My family.

My future.

My fear.

My ambition.

All of it.

What I was trying to say is simple:

If Christ is Lord, He cannot be a consultant.

He must be King.

---

## What I Actually Lived While Preaching It

While I was preaching, I realized surrender is not something I can explain from a distance.

I have to live it while I speak it.

That means every sermon becomes a mirror.

I cannot preach surrender and still hold back the parts of myself I do not want God to touch.

I cannot preach surrender and still try to control the outcome.

I cannot preach surrender and still make the message about me.

The sermon exposed me while I was preaching it.

That is the power of the Word.

It does not just go outward.

It comes back inward.

---

## What I Got Right

I believe I got the heart of the message right.

Surrender is not weakness.

Surrender is trust.

Surrender is faith with full weight placed on Christ.

I also believe I made the message personal enough for people to understand that God does not want a performance.

He wants the person.

He wants the whole life.

---

## What I Should Have Corrected

I should have slowed down more.

Surrender is too important to rush.

I should have spent more time helping people understand that surrender is not a one-time emotional moment.

It is daily obedience.

It is daily death to self.

It is daily trust.

I also should have made clearer that surrender is not losing your identity.

It is finally placing your identity in Christ.

---

## What I Prayed About Afterward

Lord,

Do not let me preach surrender and still live in control.

Do not let me speak about giving You everything while hiding pieces of myself.

Take what I am still holding.

Take what I am still protecting.

Take what I am still trying to manage without You.

Teach me to surrender fully.

Not once.

Daily.

In Jesus’ name,

Amen.

---

## What God Is Teaching Me Now

God is teaching me that surrender comes before everything else.

Before Mirror.

Before Water.

Before Fire.

Before public witness.

Before the Fifth Gospel can be lived, the old self has to bow.

Surrender is the doorway.

Abiding in Christ is the life that begins after surrender.

Then the mirror can expose.

Then the water can cleanse.

Then the fire can refine.

---

## The Fifth Gospel Reflection

The Fifth Gospel is not written by a man trying to impress God.

It is lived by a man who has surrendered to God.

That is what this sermon showed me.

If people are going to read Christ through my life, then my life cannot be built on control.

It has to be built on surrender.

---

## Closing Witness

I preached the sermon once.

Now I have to live it daily.
