# After Sermon 003: Mirror

## What God Showed Me After I Preached

Opening Scripture:

"But be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves." — James 1:22

---

## What I Was Trying to Say

This sermon was about the mirror.

The mirror is where I stop looking at everybody else and let the Word look at me.

Not my image.

Not my platform.

Not my intentions.

Me.

The mirror asks the question I cannot escape:

Does my life line up with the Word of God?

That is what I was trying to say.

Before I can talk about cleansing.

Before I can talk about fire.

Before I can talk about public witness.

I have to look honestly.

The mirror does not exist to shame me.

The mirror exists to reveal me.

---

## What I Actually Lived While Preaching It

While I was preaching, I realized the mirror is uncomfortable because it does not let me hide behind the sermon.

It is easy to preach truth outward.

It is harder to let truth come inward.

The sermon forced me to ask:

Am I living this?

Am I loving everyone?

Am I obeying what I am teaching?

Am I looking forward, or am I looking back like Lot’s wife?

That was the conviction.

I cannot get to fire if I refuse the mirror.

I cannot become the Fifth Gospel if I keep protecting the parts of myself the Word is trying to expose.

---

## What I Got Right

I believe I got the order right.

Mirror has to come before Water and Fire.

If I skip the mirror, I skip correction.

If I skip correction, I confuse passion with obedience.

If I skip obedience, I cannot reflect Christ publicly.

I also believe I got the heart right:

The mirror is love.

God does not expose me to destroy me.

He exposes me to form me.

---

## What I Should Have Corrected

I should have made clearer that the mirror is not self-hatred.

It is not obsessing over failure.

It is not living under condemnation.

The mirror is Scripture showing me where I need God.

I also should have spent more time explaining that the mirror must be held by Christ.

Without Christ, the mirror becomes shame.

With Christ, the mirror becomes grace and correction.

---

## What I Prayed About Afterward

Lord,

Show me the truth about myself.

Do not let me preach to others and avoid correction in my own life.

Hold the mirror of Your Word in front of me.

Expose what needs to be exposed.

Correct what needs to be corrected.

Keep me from looking back.

Keep me from hardening my heart.

Teach me to receive correction as love.

In Jesus’ name,

Amen.

---

## What God Is Teaching Me Now

God is teaching me that surrender brings me to the mirror.

Abiding in Christ gives me the strength to stay there.

The mirror shows me what must be cleansed.

Then the water can wash.

Then the fire can refine.

This is why the order matters.

Surrender → Abide in Christ → Mirror → Water → Fire

I cannot rush formation.

I cannot skip exposure.

I cannot become the Fifth Gospel without first letting the Word read me.

---

## The Fifth Gospel Reflection

The Fifth Gospel is not a perfect life.

It is a corrected life.

It is a life where the Word is allowed to confront, cleanse, and form the messenger.

If people are going to read Christ through me, then I cannot hide from the mirror.

I have to let God correct me before I try to correct the world.

---

## Closing Witness

I preached the sermon once.

Now I have to live it daily.
