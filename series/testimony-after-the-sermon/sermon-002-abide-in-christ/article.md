# After Sermon 002: Abide in Christ

## What God Showed Me After I Preached

Opening Scripture:

"Abide in me, and I in you." — John 15:4

---

## What I Was Trying to Say

This sermon was about abiding in Christ.

Not visiting Christ.

Not remembering Christ only when life gets heavy.

Not using Christ as a rescue plan after I have already exhausted myself.

Abiding means remaining.

It means staying connected.

It means the life I preach from must come from Him.

I was trying to say that a person can know religious language and still not abide.

A person can create content about God and still need to sit with God.

A person can preach, teach, pray, and post, but if Christ is not the source, the work becomes noise.

Abiding is the difference between performance and fruit.

---

## What I Actually Lived While Preaching It

While I was preaching, I felt the weight of my own responsibilities.

Family.

Calling.

Work.

Content.

The books.

The sermons.

The platforms.

The pressure to keep building.

And then the sermon turned back on me.

Jesus had crowds.

Jesus had demands.

Jesus had people needing healing.

Jesus had enemies watching Him.

Jesus had disciples depending on Him.

And still, Jesus went away to pray.

That convicted me.

If Jesus found time to pray, I have no excuse.

Abiding cannot be optional for me.

It has to become the root.

---

## What I Got Right

I believe I got the central warning right:

Without abiding in Christ, I remain a cultural Christian.

I may look Christian.

I may sound Christian.

I may even build Christian things.

But fruit only comes from remaining in Him.

I also believe I connected abiding to the Fifth Gospel correctly.

People cannot read Christ through my life if my life is disconnected from Christ.

---

## What I Should Have Corrected

I should have made the difference clearer between activity and intimacy.

Doing things for God is not the same as being with God.

Preaching about Christ is not the same as abiding in Christ.

Building for the Kingdom is not the same as remaining in the King.

I also should have slowed down and emphasized that abiding is not laziness.

Abiding is the source of true action.

The branch does not produce fruit by striving.

The branch produces fruit by remaining connected to the vine.

---

## What I Prayed About Afterward

Lord,

Do not let me build without abiding.

Do not let me preach without prayer.

Do not let me confuse movement with fruit.

Teach me to remain in Christ.

Teach me to sit with You before I speak for You.

Teach me to seek Your presence before I seek the platform.

Make my life rooted.

Make my witness fruitful.

In Jesus’ name,

Amen.

---

## What God Is Teaching Me Now

God is teaching me that surrender opens the door, but abiding keeps me in the house.

Surrender says:

Lord, take my life.

Abiding says:

Lord, keep me near You every day.

This comes before Mirror → Water → Fire.

Because if I do not abide, the mirror becomes self-condemnation.

If I do not abide, the water becomes self-improvement.

If I do not abide, the fire becomes burnout.

Christ must be the source.

---

## The Fifth Gospel Reflection

The Fifth Gospel is not a public image.

It is a life connected to Christ.

If my children, my family, my viewers, and strangers at The Rock are reading my life, then what they need to see is not just output.

They need to see fruit.

And fruit only comes from abiding.

---

## Closing Witness

I preached the sermon once.

Now I have to live it daily.
